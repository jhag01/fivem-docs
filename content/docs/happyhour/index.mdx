title: Overview
description: A Happy Hour system for FiveM activities, with framework support for ESX, Qbox, and standalone servers
---

happyHour lets staff temporarily boost (or penalize) XP/payout-style multipliers for specific activities — "police get 2x this weekend" or "fishing is 0.5x tonight" — through an in-game menu, with the schedule persisted across restarts and exposed to other resources via exports and events.

## Features

- **Framework-aware activities** — auto-pulls jobs from ESX or Qbox, or define your own standalone/custom activity list
- **Sad Hours** — the same multiplier field doubles as a penalty: anything below `1.0` is a debuff instead of a bonus
- **One-time or recurring** — schedule a single window, or a weekly recurring one (including windows that cross midnight)
- **Editable entries** — adjust the time or multiplier on an existing entry without deleting and recreating it
- **Notifications** — broadcast or job-filtered announcements when an entry starts or ends
- **Live hooks** — events and exports for other resources to react without polling
- **Persistent** — schedule survives a resource restart via `ResourceKvp`
- **Quick command** — `/happyhour quick <activity> <minutes> <multiplier>` for a one-off boost without opening the menu

## How it works, at a glance

Entries live in a single in-memory table on the server, persisted to a KVP on every change. A 30-second server thread does two jobs in one pass: prunes expired one-time entries, and diffs each entry's active/inactive state to fire start/end events and notifications exactly once per transition. Which activities are manageable at all comes from [Activities](/happyhour/activities/) — a merge of your framework's jobs (if any), a whitelist, and your own custom entries.
